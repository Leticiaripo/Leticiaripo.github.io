Living in the mobile device era, there should be a new type of interaction that utilizes the mobile device. Motion Silhouette is a technique created by two Japanese artists, Megumi Kajiwara and Tatsuhiko Niijima. At first glance, a book using this technique looks like a normal pop-up book; however, as the pop-up piece gets light, it projects a shadow on the other side of the page, creating a silhouette. As the reader changes the direction of the light, he silhouette moves tothe opposite page.

Using this movement of the silhouette, readers can create their own animations. This technique requires a direct light to project clear edges of the silhouettes. Most light sources used in the past emitted scattered light because these lights were designed to cover an area. On the other hand, the direct light that comes with a mobile device can create clear edges of the silhouettes given its narrow diameter. Also, compared to the heavy weight of household lights, mobile devices are easy to move around to create silhouette animations and can be held by children for increased entertainment.

Motion Silhouette is a great method to create a new type of children’s interactive book. It provides an active interaction to a contemporary child, since most parents carry mobile devices with them and hand over them to their child when they are busy. Using contemporary devices instead of playing a digital game, a child can use it to improve necessary skills that they need to learn at their age. While holding and moving a mobile device, a child improve his or her concentration, as well as improve motor skills. It also teaches high level concepts, such as spatial awareness and a relation between light and shadow indirectly. Understanding these different dimension enhances a child’s lateral thinking ability, addressing potential current issues through an indirect and creative approach.

To enrich the book experience a mobile app was created, that uses artificial intelligence techniques of image recognition to deliver background music and sound effects. As the child moves the phone and plays with the shadows our app delivers sounds effects sensible to the movement of the phone and background music to engage the reader in Nina & Cookie ’s world.
