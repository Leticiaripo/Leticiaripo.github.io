SIGGRAPH VRT 2018

The VR Theatre experience is an immersive environment to showcase curated VR films, seamlessly transporting conference attendees between the films and our virtual world.

SIGGRAPH is an annual conference on computer graphics convened by the ACM SIGGRAPH organization held since 1974. The VR theatre, part of the conference since 2017, is set up to showcase selected VR shorts that are submitted by creators around the world.

The first VR Theatre was run in 45 minute sessions with about 15 participants per session. This year’s VR theatre was aiming to increase capacity by hosting at least 35 participants.

Members of the SIGGRAPH committee sought partnership with the Centre of Digital Media in an attempt to create a more immersive experience that would also integrate this year’s theme, Generations, in a mixed reality world.

The VR Theatre experience is an immersive environment to showcase curated VR films, seamlessly transporting conference attendees between the films and our virtual world.

The team initiated the process by collaborating with the client in extensive exercises to brainstorm and make our research as valuable and relevant as possible. Prototyping in paper first, we attended various VR events in Vancouver as well as workshops to better understand deisgning in 360.

For 2018, SIGGRAPH was looking to make the overall experience more automated, user-driven, and immersive. The committee wanted to increase capacity to about 35 participants per session and make a more immersive experience - one that would include a virtual host to guide users, user interactivity, projection mapping (to make the space more dynamic), and an experience that emphasised the conference’s theme.

The committee felt last year’s experience was lacking immersion and agency because:

1. It was more of a lounge. User’s didn’t know when things started and when they ended. This year the committee wanted more of a theatrical experience.

2. VR has progressed and this year the committee wanted to showcase VR as a real platform for the mainstream audience.

3. The transition from the different realities was not smooth. The committee wanted to show how we could transition from the conference floor to the VR theatre room to VR and back. Showing the story arc of the full experience.

The team created a Design Kit to help allign on the visual design patterns between the sub-teams.

A journey map helped visualise the convergence of the physical and virtual experience of our user journey. The audience of Siggraph 2017 was thoroughly researched to create a persona from which the journey map derived.
